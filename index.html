About

As a dedicated educator and researcher, I'm deeply passionate about examining the role of career development practitioners in regional, remote, and rural communities. I bring 12 years of experience as a high school teacher to my research, providing me with invaluable insights into the practical challenges and opportunities in education. My research approach is mixed-methods, combining quantitative and qualitative techniques. I have a particular focus on reflexive thematic analysis, which allows me to uncover rich, nuanced understandings of career development in non-urban settings. Currently, I'm actively teaching in work-integrated learning, where I bridge the gap between academic theory and real-world application. This hands-on approach informs my research and helps me develop practical strategies for career practitioners. My goal is to contribute meaningful insights that can shape policy and improve career outcomes for individuals in underserved regions. I strive to translate complex research findings into actionable strategies, hoping to make a tangible difference in the field of career development.
